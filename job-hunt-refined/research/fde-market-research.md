# FDE Market Research — Live Snapshot

*Researched June 25, 2026 · open to relocating · FDE track first*

## The headline

The market is wide open and the timing is unusually good. FDE postings grew roughly 700%+ year-over-year (one tracker: ~640 to 5,300+ listings April-to-April). Both OpenAI and Anthropic launched *billion-dollar FDE ventures within days of each other in May 2026* — Anthropic's is a $1.5B JV with Blackstone, Hellman & Friedman, and Goldman, embedding engineers directly inside portfolio companies. This isn't a fad title; it's where a large chunk of AI hiring budget is actively flowing right now. You are job-hunting into a hiring surge, not a drought.

Comp is strong across the board: Google Cloud FDE ~$147K-$211K base + equity; OpenAI mid-level ~$160K-$280K base (SF); top AI labs clearing $385K-$550K+ all-in at senior. Median advertised ~$174K with equity in ~70% of offers. Even the floor here likely beats your BofA comp.

## Where you genuinely fit (and where you don't, yet)

Reading the actual JDs against your profile:

**Strong fit:**
- "Strong programming skills with proficiency in Python, experience shipping production applications" — you, directly.
- "High agency, ability to navigate ambiguity in complex organizations" — your whole instinct, and the consulting/lead-gen build is evidence.
- "Strong communication skills to convey technical concepts to diverse stakeholders" — your stated presence + people skills.
- Financial-services FDE roles specifically value domain background — your BofA + markets fluency is a *differentiator* here, not baggage. (There are explicit "Sr. FDE - Financial Services" postings.)

**The real gap — be honest about it:**
Nearly every top-tier FDE JD (Anthropic, OpenAI) now requires *"production experience with LLMs: advanced prompt engineering, agent development, evaluation frameworks, and deployment at scale."* The 2026 non-negotiables that keep recurring:
- **Agent frameworks** — LangGraph, LangChain, CrewAI, DSPy. Multi-step tool-use chains where the model calls APIs, reads DBs, writes to systems.
- **Eval engineering** — building suites that catch hallucinations/regressions/grounding gaps *before* production. This is named as *the* non-negotiable 2026 skill and it's the thing most candidates lack.
- **MCP servers, sub-agents, agent skills** — Anthropic's JD literally lists "deliver technical artifacts like MCP servers, sub-agents, and agent skills."

This is the single most important finding: **your Anchor Project 2 should be built specifically to close this gap.** An agentic tool with a real eval harness and an MCP server isn't just a portfolio piece — it's a near-literal checklist match against the Anthropic/OpenAI JDs. That's where your build energy converts most directly into interviews.

**Experience bar:** Anthropic's own listing asks 3-4+ years in a technical customer-facing role *or software engineer with consulting experience* — and explicitly "former technical founders encouraged." You don't have the "FDE" title, but "software engineer with consulting experience" is a door, and your lead-gen consulting build + BofA engineering is a credible way through it. The JDs all say apply even if you don't meet every qualification; take them at their word.

## Caveats worth knowing going in

- **Travel: 25-50%** is standard in these JDs (Anthropic 25-50%, Deloitte ~50%). Since you're open to relocating and were drawn to a life with more motion, this may be a plus rather than a cost — but know it's real and decide deliberately.
- **Remote rate is lower than general SWE** (~34% of FDE roles remote-eligible) because customer presence *is* the value. "Open to relocating" serves you well here.
- **The role is <2 yrs old as a widespread title**, so comp bands are still unstable — a startup offer can sit 30% below a later-stage one for the same work. Benchmark aggressively before signing (Levels.fyi, the FDE-specific trackers).
- **Interview shape is different**: less algorithmic LeetCode, more "decomposition" case study + deployment-under-ambiguity + communication. Anthropic's loop reportedly tests deployment thinking *instead of* heavy algorithms. So for the FDE track specifically, weight case-study rehearsal and "explain it to a non-technical VP" practice over grinding hards.
